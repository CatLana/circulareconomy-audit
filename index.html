Section 1 of 5

Brand Identity

How clearly the brand communicates its value and looks consistent across all touchpoints.

Good

Good: Proactive CSRD regulatory positioning

The inclusion of detailed content on the Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD) and 2025 compliance needs positions CEC as an essential, future-proof partner for Irish businesses facing new regulatory requirements.

Good

Good: Proven real-world expertise and industry presence

The company's leadership is active in the industry. Tim Murphy was a featured expert at the Circular Advantage networking session in Longford, which shows real involvement beyond the website.

Warning

Warning: Logo is small and low-contrast, absent from header

The CEC logo appears only in the footer and is barely visible. There is no consistent logo usage in the header, undermining brand recognition on every page load.

Fix Add a clear, accessible logo to the header. Make sure logo, colours, and typography are consistent across all pages.
Warning

Warning: No brand guidelines - inconsistent visual identity

Colour palette, typography, and tone of voice look inconsistent across pages. Dark navy headers mix with plain white sections, generic stock photography, and no consistent brand voice.

Fix A simple one-page brand guide covering colours, type choices, and tone of voice would help maintain consistency and make onboarding new content creators much faster.
Issue

Issue: Generic brand name creates SEO and recall problems

"Circular Economy Company" is a description, not a brand name. It competes directly with global organisations using the same term, making it nearly impossible to rank for the name in search or to be remembered by prospects.

Fix Create a distinct sub-brand or tagline (e.g. "CEC Ireland - Waste Intelligence") to sit alongside the generic name. This helps with recall and makes it easier to stand out in search.
Issue

Issue: No clear value proposition above the fold

The hero section shows a stock photo of a tree with "Book a Sustainability Audit Today". There is no specific claim about who CEC helps, how, or what makes them different from competitors.

Fix Replace with one clear statement, e.g. "We cut Irish businesses' waste costs by 25%, starting with one audit." Put it above the fold with a single main call to action.
Section 2 of 5

Website UX & Design

How easy the site is to use, how professional it looks, and how well it converts B2B visitors.

Good

Good: Named blue-chip client case studies add strong credibility

Abcon, Borrisoleigh Bottling, Gilead, and Maryborough House Hotel are all named clients with testimonials. Real names build real trust. This is one of CEC's strongest selling points and it deserves more visibility throughout the site, not just on a case studies page.

Warning

Warning: Too many calls to action are confusing visitors

On the homepage alone: "Book Waste Audit", "Enquiry now", "Contact Us Today", "Book Appointment", "Pre-Audit Questionnaire". When everything is a call to action, nothing is. Visitors can't tell what the main action is supposed to be.

Fix Pick one main CTA (e.g. "Get a Free Audit") and use it consistently. All other actions should look less important.
Warning

Warning: Footer copyright reads "© 2024"

A small detail, but an outdated copyright year signals to visitors that the site may not be actively maintained. In 2026, this creates a subtle negative impression during the client vetting process.

Fix Update to © 2026. Consider dynamically generating the year with a small JavaScript snippet to avoid this recurring.
Issue

Issue: Navigation is severely overcrowded

The top menu has 12+ items including a Solutions sub-menu with 11 different options. This much choice overwhelms visitors. They can't quickly find what they need. This is flagged as a UX critical issue across the wider audit data too.

Fix Consolidate to 5–6 top-level items: Home / What We Do / Case Studies / Resources / Book Audit / Contact. Remove or bundle the 11-item Solutions sub-menu.
Issue

Issue: Personal mobile numbers displayed in site header

Tim (085 255 6696) and Colin's (086 895 2680) direct personal mobile numbers are published in plain text in the header. This is unprofessional for a B2B company targeting enterprise clients and creates a single point of failure when staff change.

Fix Replace with a single central company number and a "Contact Us" CTA button. Remove personal numbers from all public-facing header areas.
Issue

Issue: Broken social media icon links render as empty squares

The social media icons in the top navigation bar render as empty squares. The icon assets are broken or failing to load, showing as blank links. This is visible to every visitor and makes the site look unfinished.

Fix Replace broken icon assets with inline SVG icons (no external dependencies) and verify all social links resolve correctly before re-publishing.
Section 3 of 5

Content & Copywriting

How clear, accurate, and persuasive the written content is for a B2B sustainability audience.

Good

Good: Good data-backed claims are already on the site

"Reduce waste costs by up to 25%" and "save up to 95% of time on waste reporting" are great selling points - but they're buried halfway down the page where most visitors won't see them.

Opportunity Move these claims into the hero section. Repeat them at key points down the page and back them up with a case study or data source.
Good

Good: Enterprise Ireland partnership adds real credibility

Being a recognised provider in the Enterprise Ireland sustainability programme is a strong signal for Irish businesses looking for sustainability support. Right now this is buried in the footer, where almost nobody sees it.

Opportunity Move the Enterprise Ireland badge to the homepage - ideally in or just below the hero - where visitors see it straight away.
Warning

Warning: Too much jargon, not enough specifics

Phrases like "unleashing tomorrow's sustainability today" and "conquer multisite waste management" don't tell buyers anything useful. Sustainability managers, CFOs, and operations directors want numbers and clear process, not slogans.

Fix Rewrite key sections with specific outcomes, timelines, and real numbers. Lead with what the client actually gets, not abstract brand language.
Issue

Issue: Visible typos on key pages damage credibility

There are multiple spelling errors on important pages: "Technoogoy" (Technology) in the Lifecycle Mapping headline; "Governence" (Governance) in both the nav and on-page; "anditment" on the About page. These are the kind of mistakes that make enterprise clients lose confidence.

Fix A 30-minute copyedit fixes all known errors. This is the single quickest thing to do and should be done first.
Section 4 of 5

SEO & Technical Readiness

How visible the site is in search, how fast it loads, and whether the technical basics are in order.

Good

Good: Technical indexing correctly configured

The website's robots.txt is set to allow full indexing and crawling by search engines (index, follow). This is the right baseline and it's correctly in place.

Warning

Warning: Blog and content library appear inactive

The site has a Blog and Videos section but no regular publishing schedule. For B2B SEO in 2026, consistent content is what makes Google rank a site for searches like "waste audit Ireland" and "CSRD compliance Ireland". These are exactly the searches CEC's buyers are making.

Fix Commit to one optimised blog post per month. Priority targets: "waste audit Ireland", "CSRD compliance Ireland", "zero waste to landfill certification".
Issue

Issue: Homepage title tag has no target keywords

The title reads simply "Homepage - Circular Economy Company". The title tag is the most important on-page SEO element and it has no target keywords at all. Every Google result is wasted real estate.

Fix Rewrite to capture relevant searches, e.g. "Waste Management & Sustainability Audits Ireland | CEC" or "CSRD Compliance & Zero Waste Certification Ireland | Circular Economy Company".
Issue

Issue: Slider Revolution plugin is slowing the site down

The site uses the Slider Revolution WordPress plugin for its hero section. This plugin loads heavy JavaScript even when the slider is not visible, slowing down mobile page loads and hurting Core Web Vitals, which Google uses as a ranking factor.

Fix Replace the Slider Revolution hero with a lightweight static HTML/CSS hero block. Remove the plugin entirely. This alone could meaningfully improve LCP (Largest Contentful Paint) scores.
Issue

Issue: No Open Graph image - blank cards on every social share

The og:image meta tag is empty across the site. When anyone shares circulareconomy.ie on LinkedIn, WhatsApp, or other platforms, it shows as a blank card with no image, which kills click-through rates and looks unprofessional.

Fix Create a branded 1200×630px OG image and define it globally. Use a page-specific OG image for key service pages. This is a 1–2 hour fix with significant impact.
Section 5 of 5

Social Media Presence

How complete and active CEC's social channels are, and how they hold up when a buyer looks them up.

Warning

Warning: Facebook and YouTube linked but activity unclear

Links exist to facebook.com/cecireland and a YouTube channel, but without regular content, these channels are more of a liability than an asset. A dormant Facebook page looks worse than no page at all when a prospect is doing their research.

Fix Focus resource entirely on LinkedIn first. Once a posting rhythm is established, repurpose content to other channels rather than creating original content for each.
Issue

Issue: LinkedIn page is incomplete and inactive

The company page at linkedin.com/company/cecireland has no description in search results. For a B2B consultancy, LinkedIn is where buyers go to check a supplier before making contact. An empty page here costs real opportunities.

Fix Fill out the LinkedIn profile: add a description, banner image, specialities, and website URL. Post 2-3 times per week for Irish sustainability managers.
Issue

Issue: @cecireland on X (Twitter) looks abandoned

The @cecireland handle exists but has no recent activity. When someone clicks the link from the website and finds nothing, it damages trust. An inactive account is worse than no account.

Fix Either reactivate with a consistent posting schedule or remove the link from the website entirely. A dead link to an inactive account is worse than no link at all.
Issue

Issue: No Instagram presence - missed visual channel

For sustainability brands, Instagram can be effective for showing on-site audit work, environmental impact visuals, and team culture. There is no link or mention of an Instagram account, and no evidence of a presence on the platform.

Fix Once LinkedIn is active and consistent, explore Instagram as a secondary channel. Visual content (audit site photos, impact before/after) can build both brand awareness and backlinks.

Priority Actions

Eight actions ordered by impact and ease. The first three can be done in under two hours.

  1. 1

    Fix the three copy errors first: "Technoogoy", "Governence", "anditment" - a 30-minute job that makes a big difference to first impressions for any enterprise visitor.

  2. 2

    Rewrite the hero section with a clear value statement (e.g. "Cut your waste costs by 25%"), one main CTA ("Book Free Audit"), and remove personal mobile numbers from the header.

  3. 3

    Fix the broken social media icon links in the header nav. Replace the broken assets with inline SVGs. These are visible to every visitor and make the site look unfinished.

  4. 4

    Complete the LinkedIn company page with a full profile, banner image, and post 2-3 times per week for Irish sustainability managers and procurement teams.

  5. 5

    Cut the navigation from 12+ items to 5-6 clear sections (Home / What We Do / Case Studies / Resources / Book Audit / Contact) and remove the duplicate footer links.

  6. 6

    Update the homepage title tag (e.g. "Waste Management & Sustainability Audits Ireland | CEC") and add a branded OG image across key pages so social shares show a proper preview.

  7. 7

    Move the Enterprise Ireland partnership badge onto the homepage, in or just below the hero, where visitors see it from the first second.

  8. 8

    Publish one optimised blog post per month targeting: "waste audit Ireland", "CSRD compliance Ireland", "zero waste to landfill certification" - the searches CEC's buyers are making.
