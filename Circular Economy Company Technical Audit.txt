1. BRAND IDENTITY

ISSUEGeneric brand name creates SEO and recall problems.
"Circular Economy Company" is a descriptor, not a brand. It competes with global organisations using the same term Ś making it near-impossible to rank for the name organically or be remembered by prospects. Recommended fix: develop a distinctive sub-brand or tagline (e.g. "CEC Ireland ¢ Waste Intelligence") alongside the generic name.ISSUENo clear value proposition above the fold.
The hero section shows a stock photo of a tree with "Book a Sustainability Audit Today" Ś no specific claim about who they help, how, or why CEC is different. Fix: a single punchy statement e.g. "We cut Irish businesses' waste costs by 25% Ś starting with one audit."WARNINGLogo is small and low-contrast.
The CEC logo appears only in the footer and is barely visible. There is no consistent logo usage in the header. Fix: add a clear logo to the header and ensure brand consistency across all pages.WARNINGNo brand guidelines visible.
Colour palette, typography, and tone of voice appear inconsistent across pages Ś mixing dark navy headers with plain white sections, generic stock photography, and no clear brand voice. A simple one-page brand guide would help maintain consistency.

2. WEBSITE UX & DESIGN

ISSUENavigation is severely overcrowded.
The top menu has 12+ items including a Solutions sub-menu with 11 items. This causes decision paralysis Ś visitors don't know where to go. Fix: consolidate into 5¢6 top-level items: Home / What We Do / Case Studies / Resources / Book Audit / Contact.ISSUEPersonal phone numbers in the site header.
Tim and Colin's direct mobile numbers are published in plain text in the header (085 255 6696, 086 895 2680). This is unprofessional for a B2B company targeting enterprise clients, and creates a single point of failure if staff change. Fix: use a single company number with a contact form CTA  instead.ISSUEBroken icon links in the header.
The social media icons in the top navigation bar render as empty squares Ś the icon assets are broken or not loading, displaying as blank links. This needs an immediate fix as it looks unfinished to all visitors.WARNINGMultiple CTAs compete for attention.
On the homepage alone: "Book Waste Audit", "Enquiry now", "Contact Us Today", "Book Appointment", "Pre-Audit Questionnaire". The primary action is unclear. Fix: choose one primary CTA (e.g. "Get a Free Audit") and use it consistently throughout.WARNINGCopyright footer reads "® 2024".
Small detail, but signals to visitors the site may not be actively maintained. Update to ® 2026.GOODNamed client case studies add strong credibility.
Abcon, Borrisoleigh Bottling, Gilead, and Maryborough House Hotel Ś real named clients with testimonials. This is a genuine differentiator for CEC and should be more prominent throughout the site.

3. CONTENT & COPYWRITING

ISSUECopy contains visible typos and grammar errors.
"Technoogoy" (should be Technology) in the Lifecycle Mapping headline. "ESG ¢ Environmental, Social & Governence" (misspelled) appears in both the nav and on-page. "anditment" on the About page. These undermine credibility on a B2B site targeting enterprise buyers. Fix: a 30-minute copyedit would resolve all known errors.WARNINGToo much jargon, too little specificity.
Phrases like "unleashing tomorrow's sustainability today" and "conquer multisite waste management" don't communicate concrete outcomes. Target buyers Ś sustainability managers, CFOs, operations directors Ś want numbers and process clarity, not marketing slogans.GOODStrong anchor claims already exist.
"Reduce waste costs by up to 25%" and "save up to 95% of time on waste reporting" are excellent if substantiated. These should be moved into the hero section and used more prominently throughout rather than buried mid-page.GOODEnterprise Ireland partnership is a strong trust signal.
Being a recognised service provider to the Enterprise Ireland sustainability programme is highly credible with Irish SMEs and enterprises. Currently buried in the footer Ś it should appear prominently on the homepage, ideally in the hero or just below it.

4. SEO & TECHNICAL

ISSUEHomepage title tag is wasted.
The title reads simply "Homepage ¢ Circular Economy Company". This is the most important on-page SEO element and it includes no target keywords. Fix: rewrite to something like "Waste Management & Sustainability Audits Ireland | CEC" to capture relevant search traffic.ISSUEBuilt on Slider Revolution Ś a known performance bottleneck.
The site uses the Slider Revolution WordPress plugin for its hero section. This plugin is notorious for loading heavy JavaScript even when sliders are not visible, significantly slowing mobile page loads and hurting Core Web Vitals Ś a direct Google ranking factor since 2021.ISSUENo OG image defined for social sharing.
The meta og:image tag is empty across the site. When anyone shares circulareconomy.ie on LinkedIn, WhatsApp, or other platforms, it will show a blank card with no image. This dramatically reduces click-through rates from social shares.WARNINGBlog and content library appear inactive.
The site has a Blog and Videos section but no visible regular publishing cadence. For B2B SEO in 2026, consistent content is essential for ranking for terms like "waste audit Ireland" and "CSRD compliance Ireland" Ś exactly the searches CEC's target buyers are making.GOODFull site indexing is correctly enabled.
The robots.txt is set to index, follow Ś the site is fully crawlable. This is a baseline requirement that is correctly in place.

5. SOCIAL MEDIA

ISSUELinkedIn page appears incomplete and dormant.
The company page at linkedin.com/company/cecireland yields no description in search snippets Ś a sign of an incomplete or inactive profile. For a B2B sustainability consultancy, LinkedIn is the primary channel and needs to be treated as a sales asset. Target buyers actively use it to vet suppliers.ISSUE@cecireland on X (Twitter) appears abandoned.
The handle exists but shows no recent activity. An abandoned social account creates a negative impression Ś visitors who click the link from the website expect to see an active brand. Either reactivate or remove the link from the site.WARNINGFacebook and YouTube linked but activity unclear.
Links exist to facebook.com/cecireland and a YouTube channel. Without regular content these channels add little value and create another touchpoint that may look neglected. Focus resource on LinkedIn first, then repurpose content to other channels.ISSUENo Instagram presence.
For sustainability brands, Instagram can be effective for showing on-site audit work, environmental impact visuals, and team culture. There is no link or mention of an account, and no evidence of a presence. Worth exploring as a secondary channel once LinkedIn is active.

PRIORITY ACTIONS

The following eight actions are ordered by impact and ease of execution:
1. Fix the three copy errors immediately: "Technoogoy", "Governence", "anditment" Ś a 30-minute fix that has an outsized impact on perceived professionalism.
2. Rewrite the hero section with a clear value proposition, a single CTA ("Book Free Audit"), and remove personal mobile numbers from the header.
3. Fix the broken social media icon links in the header navigation Ś these are visible to every visitor.
4. Rebuild the LinkedIn company page with a complete profile, banner image, and commit to 2¢3 posts per week targeting Irish sustainability managers.
5. Simplify navigation from 12+ items to 5¢6 clear sections and remove the duplicate LinkedIn link in the footer.
6. Update the homepage title tag and add OG images across all key pages to enable rich social share previews.
7. Move the Enterprise Ireland partnership badge prominently onto the homepage Ś above the fold or in the hero section.
8. Publish one optimised blog post per month targeting: "waste audit Ireland", "CSRD compliance Ireland", "zero waste to landfill certification".
