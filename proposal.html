The reasoning

Why this plan is the recommended way to go

Why a rebuild, not a patch. The current website runs on Slider Revolution, a heavy WordPress plugin that was built for visual effects, not performance. It slows every page load, limits what you can do with SEO and analytics, and makes even small changes complicated. Trying to improve results on that foundation is like decorating a room with a cracked floor. A clean rebuild takes weeks, not months, and gives you a site that is properly optimised from the start rather than something that needs constant workarounds.

Why analytics must come before paid ads. If you run Google Ads or any other paid campaign on a site with no conversion tracking, you have no way of knowing which clicks led to enquiries and which ones cost money for nothing. Setting up analytics first means that when you do spend on ads, every euro is tracked back to a real outcome. Without that baseline, you are spending blind.

Why SEO is a monthly job, not a one-time fix. Getting into Google does not happen after a single round of changes. Search rankings are earned over time through consistent, relevant content, a technically sound website, and regular adjustments based on what is and is not working. A monthly retainer keeps that process running. A one-off job delivers an initial boost and then fades without ongoing attention.

One team, one brief, no gaps. The most common problem I see is a developer who built the site and a marketer who was brought in later, neither of whom knows the full picture. The developer does not think about the sales funnel. The marketer cannot change the site. The result is a website that looks fine but does not convert, and a marketing spend that pushes people to a page that lets them down. Having one provider responsible for the website, the message, and the marketing means none of those gaps exist. When something is not working, there is one person to call.
