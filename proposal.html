The bigger picture

Why staying the same costs more than changing

Your website is losing you enquiries right now. Every week, potential customers land on a slow, hard-to-navigate site and leave without getting in touch. There is no error message, no notification, just silence. You cannot see what you are missing. But it is happening.

There is no neutral position. Doing nothing is not free. You are already paying for hosting, your domain, and potentially some form of support. The question is not whether to spend money on your digital presence, it is whether that spend is producing anything. Right now, the honest answer is that it is not.

Your competitors are moving. As more businesses improve their websites and their online visibility, the bar that customers expect goes up. Standing still while others invest means falling behind in relative terms, even if nothing changes on your side. The gap compounds over time.

The longer you wait, the more it will cost. SEO is built over time. A domain with no fresh content and no inbound links starts to slide in search rankings gradually. Rebuilding from a weaker position takes more time than maintaining from a strong one. Starting now means you reach results sooner and at lower total cost.

The reasoning

Why this is the right approach

Why a rebuild, not a patch. The current website runs on Slider Revolution, a heavy WordPress plugin built for visual effects, not performance. It slows every page load, limits what you can do with SEO and analytics, and makes even small changes complicated. Trying to improve results on that foundation is like decorating a room with a cracked floor. A clean rebuild takes weeks, not months, and gives you a site that is properly optimised from the start.

Why analytics must come before paid ads. If you run Google Ads on a site with no conversion tracking, you have no way of knowing which clicks led to enquiries and which ones cost money for nothing. Setting up analytics first means that when you do spend on ads, every euro is traceable back to a real outcome.

Why SEO needs ongoing attention. Getting into Google does not happen after a single round of changes. Rankings are earned over time through consistent content, a technically sound website, and regular adjustments based on what is and is not working. A monthly plan keeps that process running. A one-off job produces an initial improvement and then fades without follow-through.

One team, one brief, no gaps. The most common problem we see is a developer who built the site and a marketer who was brought in later, with neither knowing the full picture. The developer does not think about the sales funnel. The marketer cannot change the site. The result is a website that looks fine but does not convert. Having one provider responsible for the website, the message, and the marketing means none of those gaps exist.
