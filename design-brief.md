## 2. Competitor Landscape

### Large Irish waste operators
Panda (Beauparc Group), Thorntons, Oxenvale. These are **collection and haulage businesses** — high volume, impersonal, zero audit methodology. They are CEC's indirect competition: clients use them but have no insight into what they spend or waste.

### Global consultancies
Bain & Company, PwC, BCG, SLR Consulting, Stena Circular Consulting. These serve **multinationals with large teams**. Too expensive and generic for Irish mid-market. Not the same buyer.

### Boutique circular economy specialists
- **GATE C** (France, est. 2012): 300+ consulting projects globally, dedicated circular economy only — closest international peer. No Irish presence.
- **Circle Economy** (Netherlands): works with UN, WEF. Global scale, not local delivery.
- **Stena Circular Consulting** (Sweden): recycling group with advisory arm. Industrial focus.

### The gap CEC owns
**There is no other Irish boutique that combines:** a structured waste audit + proprietary software (Eiravato) + personal expert-level delivery for mid-to-large Irish businesses. That is CEC's genuine, ownable positioning — and no competitor currently occupies it in Ireland.

**CEC's decisive differentiator:** Other consultants ask you to commit to a solution before understanding your problem. CEC measures first. The audit is not a sales exercise — it is the product. This creates a clear, specific, fundable plan which competitors cannot match without doing the same work.

## 4. Content Structure — Sales Flow

The landing page follows: **We are → Who we help → Through what → What we offer → Why us → Proof → CTA**

1. **Nav** — minimal: Logo | What We Do | How It Works | Case Studies | Contact | CTA button
2. **Hero** — outcome headline + who/how subhead + single CTA + trust strip (25%, 95%, 9 years) + Enterprise Ireland badge
3. **Who we help** — 3 columns: sector / role / situation
4. **How it works** — 3 steps: Audit → Plan → Results. Led by "We know where to start."
5. **What you gain** — 5 benefit cards: costs down / time saved / CSRD / ZWtL / ESG rating
6. **Why CEC** — 4 differentiators: audit-first / Eiravato data / senior delivery / Irish expertise
7. **Case studies** — 3 compact cards: client, sector, outcome → link to full case study
8. **Testimonial** — Maryborough House Hotel Financial Controller
9. **CTA section** — "Book a free waste audit. No obligation."
10. **Footer** — minimal

## 5. Colour Palette

**Rationale:** The standard "recycling-bin green" signals eco charity, not business expertise. CFOs and sustainability managers at blue-chip companies respond to authority first, green credentials second. The palette below sits in the same register as top-tier ESG consulting brands — serious and credible, with enough green to signal the mission without being generic.

| Role | Name | Hex | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary | Deep forest | `#1B4332` | Authority + genuine sustainability |
| Mid green | Forest mid | `#2D6A4F` | Hover states, gradient depth |
| Light accent | Sage | `#74A887` | Data callouts, icons on dark |
| Background | Warm linen | `#F7F4EE` | Off-white, premium, not clinical |
| Background dark | Linen deep | `#EDE9E0` | Section dividers, borders |
| Accent | Copper brass | `#B87333` | Distinctive, premium, Irish connotation |
| Accent light | Copper light | `#D4924A` | Hover, number highlights |
| Text | Near-black | `#111827` | Body text |
| Muted | Stone | `#6B7280` | Secondary / supporting copy |
| White | White | `#FFFFFF` | Clean section backgrounds |

**Avoid:** Bright lime greens (`#00FF00`, `#7CFC00`), white + grey only (generic SaaS), black + neon (startup).

## 6. Typography

**Direction:** Authority serif headline + clean sans-serif body. Used by premium consulting and financial brands. Signals expertise, not trendy tech. Perfectly legible for time-pressed C-suite readers who scan.

| Role | Font | Weight | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Display / H1–H2 | **Fraunces** (Google Fonts, free, variable) | 700–800 | Variable serif with editorial weight. Fraunces is used by premium consulting and financial brands. Signals expertise. |
| Body / H3–H6, UI | **Inter** (already in project) | 400–600 | Highly legible, neutral, universal trust. Already loaded — no additional weight. |
| Data callouts | Inter | 700–800, tabular | Numbers need to feel precise |

**Alternative display font:** DM Serif Display — cleaner, more corporate if Fraunces reads too editorial for client.

**Type scale:**
- Hero H1: `clamp(2.75rem, 5vw, 4.5rem)` — commanding, readable on mobile
- Section H2: `clamp(2rem, 3.5vw, 2.875rem)` — clear hierarchy
- H3 / Card titles: `1.25–1.375rem`
- Body: `1rem` (16px base), line-height `1.6–1.65`
- Captions / tags: `0.75–0.8125rem`

## 7. Design Direction

**Paradigm:** Premium B2B consulting. Not green charity. Not SaaS startup.

**Reference brands:** Deloitte Sustainability, McKinsey sustainability pages, AIB Business Banking — clean authority with warm undertones.

**Photography:** Never stock photos of leaves, trees, or handshakes. Use real photography from client sites (production floors, data screens) or clean abstract data visualisations. A real photo from Abcon's facility is worth more than any stock image.

**Layout principles:**
- Generous white space. C-suite readers scan, not read. White space signals confidence.
- Data-forward. Large numbers (25%, 95%) treated as design elements, not footnotes.
- One primary CTA per section. The current site's 5 competing CTAs dilute every page.
- Trust marks visible above the fold. Enterprise Ireland badge, 9 years, named clients.
- Alternating section backgrounds (forest dark / white / linen) create rhythm without complexity.

**Section backgrounds:**
- Hero: deep forest green (`#1B4332`)
- Who we help: warm linen
- How it works: white
- Benefits: warm linen
- Why CEC: deep forest green (dark sections reinforce authority)
- Case studies: white
- Testimonial: warm linen
- CTA: deep forest green
- Footer: near-black
